Title: “The Obsession” David, a lonely and introverted man in his early 30s, works as a data analyst in a small office. He leads a monotonous, solitary life, spending his days buried in numbers and his nights staring at the profiles of women on social media. One day, he meets Anna, a charming and beautiful woman who works at a coffee shop next door. Over time, David visits the café daily, subtly building a connection with her, convinced she’s the one person who truly understands him.

As their interactions grow more frequent, David’s obsession with Anna deepens. He begins to follow her online, tracking her movements and obsessing over every detail of her life. But when David learns that Anna is engaged to another man, his world begins to unravel. The realization sends him into a spiral of jealousy and rage, leading him to confront Anna, hoping to convince her to leave her fiancé and be with him instead.

When she rejects him, David’s fixation turns dangerous. Desperate and unable to cope with the rejection, David retreats into a new obsession: creating a life-sized doll that he molds and dresses to resemble Anna. He pours all his energy into crafting the perfect replica—painting the doll’s face, styling its hair, and even dressing it in the same clothes Anna wears when she visits the café. Over time, he begins to treat the doll as if it were Anna, talking to it, confiding in it, and imagining that it responds to him.

The doll becomes his twisted surrogate for the real Anna, a way to feel as though he has control over her and their relationship. He speaks to it as though it were alive, sharing his every thought, and even attempts to mimic Anna’s voice and actions when alone with the doll. But his obsession grows darker as he begins to believe the doll is more than just a lifeless object—it is his only connection to Anna, and through it, he feels empowered.

David’s growing obsession with the doll takes a violent turn when he realizes he must eliminate the man standing in his way. He stalks Anna’s fiancé, and after a brutal confrontation, he decides that Anna, too, must be brought under his control. As his sanity deteriorates, David’s fantasy world becomes more chaotic and his grip on reality more tenuous. His obsession with Anna and the doll consumes him entirely, leading him down a path of irreversible destruction.

Themes: Obsession, loneliness, rejection, control, delusion, psychological decline, and the blurred line between reality and fantasy.

The doll serves as both a symbol of David’s desperate need for control and a manifestation of his unhealthy fixation on Anna, illustrating how far he’s willing to go to construct a reality where he holds power over her.
